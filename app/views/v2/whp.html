Use nature based solutions to reduce flooding

Find out how the risk of flooding in your area can be reduced by using natural flood management.

From:
Department for Environment, Food and Rural Affairs
Published:
28 March 2021

Communities can reduce flood risk by restoring natural features to the landscape such as ponds and woodland to slow the flow of rainfall into streams and rivers. On the coast these techniques can reduce the risk of flooding from waves, tidal and storm surges.

Slowing and storing water upstream so that it takes longer to enter the river system can make flooding less likely in villages and towns downstream.

How your community can benefit

Using nature based solutions can provide benefits for communities especially those who live in flood risk areas.

It can:

  • delay and reduce the impact of flooding
  • increase the range of plant and animal species
  • improve water quality in local rivers and streams
  • store carbon in trees and plants to help reduce global warming

What communities can do to reduce flood risk

Communities can use a range of approaches to naturally manage the flow of water in their local landscape.

You can:

  • plant trees to increase water absorption
  • improve soil cover to reduce water pollution and run off
  • dig ponds to divert and store water
  • encourage natural flooding in safe areas
  • restore salt marshes, mudflats and peat bogs
  • build sand dunes

Find out about the different types of natural flood management suitable for different areas.

Sustainable drainage for homes and business premises

Buildings and paving can increase the risk of floods. When it rains the water is not absorbed by materials such as concrete and tarmac; this can cause localised surface flooding or flow directly into drains and waterways causing flooding elsewhere.

Small changes help water to drain away reducing the risk of sewer flooding during heavy rain, for example installing water butts or reducing hard paving around your property.

Find out how to use sustainable drainage.

What you need to consider

Find out about the types of natural flood management suitable for different areas.

Depending on the type of natural flood management and scale you will need to check:

  • if there is land you can use
  • if funding is available
  • if permits are required
  • how to look after and maintain anything put in place

Activities such as building large ponds and earth banks can require heavy earth moving equipment, expert advice and permits.
